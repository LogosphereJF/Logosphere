    About me

    I have a full degree in Pure Mathematics and another one in Theoretical Physics. I am an active researcher in the fields of Mathematical Physics and Topology. I've been teaching Physics and Mathematics for six years, including giving online lessons worldwide.

    I believe that teaching is about enabling your student to find and understand their own solution, rather than to simply provide them with an automatized, rigid, and artificial approach to an abstract problem.

    For this reason, I pay special attention to whether my students understand the underlying concepts and how flexible they are in dealing with them. The best results come from real interest in the subject. Hopefully I can help you find a new spark of curiosity :)

    About the lessons

    When you schedule your first lesson, feel free to send me an email in which you describe the field that we will be working in, or more specifically, the problem you are struggling with. This way, I can prepare for our meeting.

    Alternatively, before scheduling your first lesson, book a free 30-minute trial meeting. During the trial we get to know each other, we discuss your topic, and make a rough plan for our cooperation. Of course, there is no obligation to book a lesson after the trial.

    I like to use digital whiteboard tools, such as MS OneNote so that I can send you a PDF file with the notes after our lesson. If you have a graphics tablet, and you are comfortable with writing, we may use an interactive whiteboard (e.g. Miro/MS Whiteboard). If needed, I also prepare textbook materials for our lessons.
